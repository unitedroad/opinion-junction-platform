Guns n’ Roses rock Delhi

Prince of England | 12.12.12

They came, they saw and THEY ROCKED THE PLACE! Guns n’ Roses played at Delhi and it was simply the most amazing performance that we have ever seen.

Axl Rose was at his sublime best. He sounded even better than he did during the Golden Era of Guns n’ Roses. That, along with the chemistry between the band made it a night to remember for all of the crowd. Axl thoroughly entertained the crowd and played pranks, hiding behind the drums and saying “Axl Rose has gone to sleep” when he wanted to the crowd to raise their tempo. Then he put on a funny mask and saying “Axl has left the stage” while playing “Knocking on Heaven’s door”, which was longest song they played and had the crowd singing with him the whole while. The place just went berserk when they played Sweet Child ‘o Mine, and Paradise City.

It is a pity that the most in the crowd didn’t know the Chinese Democracy songs, but they really took to it when Guns n’ Roses played “Better”. We heard from the others who had come to the concert they really liked Street of Dreams too.

Once they had played the Paradise City, their staple Encore and bowed to the crowd and walked off, the thoroughly entertained but un-satiated crowd demanded more and Axl came back and asked if the crowd wants the band to play a couple of more songs.

He told them that the sound system might be off since everyone started sounding down at the end of the show and he said he apologises in advance if it doesn’t work well (!!).
The band started playing “There was a time” but then Axl knew the crowd which hasn’t heard the Chinese Democracy wouldn’t dig that so he stopped that and they started playing Nice Boys don’t play rock and roll. And we ended up with a show that was longer than the ones in Bombay and Bangalore.

Their were some delays before Axl started singing while the the band started playing the song, and for a moment we thought we will witness St Louis 2, but Axl finally came back to sing and we saw our fears were unfounded. Tommy was really pissed off since his guitar didn’t sound right and threw on stage and barged off. Then Ron picked it up and tried to play his parts but he gave it up to go back to playing his own guitar when he knew it wasn’t working.

At the end of that song, Axl played the peacemaker when he knew the sound system wasn’t holding up and told the frenzied crowd they couldn’t play another song. He thanked the crowd and they left us with amazing memories we are going to cherish for the rest of our lives.

He dedicated the concert to Ravi Shankar who passed away on the same day.

Since we were in the platinum section, which was further away from the stage than the Gold section, we couldn’t click good pictures of the show, but we will share some pictures from other sources on the internet.
